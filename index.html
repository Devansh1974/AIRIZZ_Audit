Section 01
UX / CRO Audit
Analysed from the homepage screenshot and the live site at airizz.co. Issues are grouped by category and rated High / Medium / Low impact on conversions.
🚨
The single biggest problem
The website has zero client evidence — no testimonials, no case study metrics, no real client logos. "Trusted by innovative startups…" is a claim with no proof. For a B2B AI consultancy, trust IS the entire sale. Fix this before anything else.
1 · First Impression & Hero Section
High Impact
Value proposition is generic — not specific to any buyer
"Accelerate Your Growth with AI, Data, and Automation" could describe 500 companies. There is no clear "who-for" or unique hook in the 3-second scan window. A visitor can't self-identify or feel spoken to.
Fix: Rewrite to a specific outcome for a specific buyer — e.g. "We help Indian SMBs cut manual work by 40% using AI agents — in 30 days." One sentence. Specific. Verifiable. Add one concrete proof point immediately below the headline.
High Impact
Two equal-weight CTAs split visitor intent
"Get Your Free Strategy Audit" and "Explore Our Services" sit side-by-side with near-identical visual weight. The paradox of choice means many visitors click neither. This is a direct conversion killer.
Fix: Make "Get Free Strategy Audit" the single dominant CTA (high-contrast filled button). Demote "Explore Services" to a plain text link below it. One primary action per screen.
Medium
Stats counter shows "0" on load — and numbers are weak
The animated counters (25%, 4+, 1X) appear to start from 0 and may not trigger for all users/browsers. Worse, "4 Enterprise Deployments" signals a very early-stage company. These numbers hurt more than help.
Fix: Pre-render the final values. Replace with outcome metrics: "₹2Cr+ revenue unlocked," "avg. 38% cost reduction," "3 industries served." Even small numbers are powerful when they're outcome-based.
2 · Visual Design & Hierarchy
Medium
Light gray hero background kills contrast and energy
The hero sits on near-white with light text badges. The "Enterprise-Grade AI Solutions" tag is nearly invisible. There is no visual drama — nothing tells the eye where to look first. The site reads as low-effort.
Fix: Either go dark (navy/charcoal background with white text — high contrast, premium feel) OR keep white and boost all contrast ratios to WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1). Check every text element with a contrast checker.
Medium
All 4 service cards look identical — no hierarchy or priority
The 2×2 service card grid has identical size, color, icon weight, and layout. No visual signal guides the eye to the most important or most popular service. All services appear equally valuable — which means none stands out.
Fix: Designate one "featured" service with an accent color or border. Add a one-line outcome stat to each card ("avg. 3-week delivery," "ROI in 60 days"). Let the visual hierarchy reflect your business priorities.
Low
Industry marquee looks like a logo strip — but has no logos
The scrolling ticker of industry names (Manufacturing, Healthcare, Finance…) visually resembles a client logo strip but contains only text words. It creates an expectation (client logos) and then disappoints. Reads as filler content.
Fix: Replace with 4–6 real client logos, OR remove entirely. If keeping: add a heading ("Industries we've transformed") and link each name to a relevant case study or industry landing page.
3 · Functional Flow & Cognitive Load
High Impact
Zero social proof anywhere on the homepage
No testimonials, no case study results, no client logos, no named references. "Trusted by innovative startups" is an unverifiable claim. B2B buyers — especially in India — run due diligence before booking a call. No proof = no trust = no lead.
Fix: Add minimum: 2–3 named client quotes (LinkedIn-verifiable names), OR 1 short case study card with a before/after metric. Even anonymised ("E-commerce client, Mumbai — 62% order processing reduction") beats zero evidence.
High Impact
About, Careers, Privacy, Terms — all footer links are dead (#)
All four company links in the footer redirect to "#" — they go nowhere. This is one of the first things a serious enterprise buyer checks. A broken link kills credibility instantly. It also signals the site is unfinished.
Fix: Either build these pages immediately or remove the links. Start with: About page (team bios + story) and Privacy Policy (auto-generate using a GDPR generator tool). A missing page is better than a broken link.
Medium
No pricing signal — creates budget anxiety for buyers
B2B buyers self-qualify on price. Without any indication (even "projects from ₹X" or "investment varies by scope"), visitors can't determine if AIRIZZ is in their budget. They will leave rather than email to ask — especially first-time visitors.
Fix: Add a simple 3-tier pricing card or an "investment starts from ₹X" line on each service page. Or frame it as: "Get a scoped proposal in 24 hours." Even a range removes the anxiety.
Medium
"Login" in nav goes to the contact page — misleading
The navigation shows a "Login" link that redirects to /contact. Any visitor expecting a client portal or account dashboard will be confused. It implies a SaaS product feature that doesn't exist yet.
Fix: Remove "Login" from the nav entirely until a real client portal exists. Its presence creates expectations you can't currently fulfill, which erodes trust.
4 · Mobile Optimization
High Impact
Industry tabs will break on screens under 480px
The "Solutions by Industry" section has 3 horizontal tabs (Traditional SME / Legal & Finance / Manufacturing & Logistics). On small screens these will overlap, truncate text, or stack awkwardly — making the section unusable on mobile.
Fix: Convert the horizontal tab row to a vertical accordion component on mobile (breakpoint: max-width: 768px). Each industry panel expands/collapses on tap. This is also better for accessibility.
Medium
Hero dashboard image will scale poorly on mobile
The right-side dashboard mockup in the hero is a detailed, pixel-rich image. On mobile it'll either become too small to read OR push the headline/CTA below the fold — meaning the most important action (book a call) requires scrolling before it's visible.
Fix: Hide the dashboard image on mobile (display: none below 768px) or replace with a simplified 1-metric preview. The primary CTA must be visible without scrolling on all screen sizes — this is non-negotiable.
Low
4-column service grid may stack oddly at narrow widths
The 2×2 service card grid on mobile may collapse to a single-column stack with excessive whitespace between cards, making the page feel very long and unstructured. Cards may also have inconsistent heights if content differs.
Fix: Test at 375px and 414px widths (iPhone SE and iPhone 14 sizes). Use gap: 12px and consistent card padding. Ensure icon + title + description + link are tightly grouped per card with no orphaned elements.

Section 04
What to Add — Inspired by TCS, Kalvium Labs & Top Consultancies
Based on what works at TCS, Kalvium Labs, Kearney, Slalom, and top AI agency sites. Every item is adapted for a startup budget — no enterprise spend required.
01
Outcome-first case studies with hard metrics
Kalvium Labs leads every case study with a number: "1.5 lakh users, MVP in 3 weeks." Top consultancy sites lead with "Increased revenue 34% in 6 months." Add 2–3 case study cards on the homepage — each showing: client industry, the problem, your solution, and the measurable result. Link to full case study pages. This is the single highest-ROI content investment for a consulting site.
↑ Inspired by: Kalvium Labs + Slalom + LeewayHertz case study formats
02
Thought leadership / insights blog
TCS Interactive leads with AI research reports. Slalom's hero features a thought leadership headline about "The ambition-to-execution gap." A blog with 6–10 in-depth articles ("How SMBs can cut invoice processing by 70% with AI") positions AIRIZZ as an authority, drives organic SEO traffic, and gives your sales team content to share with prospects. Publish 2 articles/month consistently for 6 months and your lead quality will improve dramatically.
↑ Inspired by: TCS Interactive research hub + SynkrAI's high-traffic blog
03
Team / About page with real faces and bios
Kalvium Labs shows founder photos, LinkedIn links, and refreshingly honest bios ("We started as an education company, realized we had great AI talent, and decided to put it to work"). B2B buyers are buying people, not logos — especially for a consulting engagement. A real About page with photos, backgrounds, and a founding story builds more trust than any other page on the site. It also differentiates you from faceless offshore dev shops.
↑ Inspired by: Kalvium Labs About page — currently the gold standard for Indian AI boutiques
04
Interactive free tool / ROI calculator on the homepage
Kalvium Labs embeds a free AI-powered PRD generator on their homepage (powered by Claude) — it's the stickiest element on their site, drives engagement, and captures warm leads. AIRIZZ could build a "How much are you losing to manual tasks?" calculator: ask 5 questions (team size, hours on manual tasks, average salary), show estimated annual cost, then CTA to book a call. Creates personalised urgency and is highly shareable on LinkedIn.
↑ Inspired by: Kalvium Labs' homepage PRD generator + Board of Innovation's frameworks
05
Industry-specific landing pages (not just tabs)
TCS structures navigation around client needs: "Your industry → Your needs." AIRIZZ has industry tabs on the homepage — extend these into full dedicated pages: /industries/legal-finance, /industries/traditional-sme, /industries/manufacturing. Each page should have: the specific pain points of that industry, AIRIZZ's tailored solutions, relevant case studies, and a CTA specific to that vertical. These pages are also excellent for SEO ("AI consulting for legal firms India").
↑ Inspired by: TCS's industry-first navigation structure
06
Scattered social proof — not just a single testimonials section
Top consultancy sites treat social proof as seasoning: a quote next to the contact form, a result stat next to each service card, a client logo by the CTA button, a metric in the hero subheadline. Rather than burying testimonials on a separate page nobody visits, weave them throughout every section. Kearney does this masterfully — their centennial messaging ("100 years of impact") is itself social proof at every scroll point.
↑ Inspired by: Kearney + Knapsack Creative's "social proof as seasoning" framework
07
Direct calendar booking — replace the contact form
Replace (or supplement) the generic contact form with a Calendly or Cal.com embed on the contact page. Let visitors book a 30-minute "AI Audit Call" without any back-and-forth emails. SynkrAI offers "your automation roadmap in 48 hours" via direct booking; Kalvium Labs uses the same approach. Direct booking converts 3–5x better than a contact form for consulting services because it removes the friction of waiting for a reply email.
↑ Used by: SynkrAI, Kalvium Labs, and essentially every top AI boutique
08
Free lead magnet — downloadable resource gated by email
Offer a downloadable PDF in exchange for an email address: "AI Readiness Checklist for Indian SMBs" or "5 Automation Wins in Under 30 Days." Gate it with a simple name + email form. Your email list is your biggest long-term asset — even 200 subscribers opens retargeting campaigns, nurture sequences, and direct outreach. A lead magnet converts passive readers into identifiable warm prospects even when they're not ready to buy today.
↑ Standard practice at: Consulting Success, BOI, most high-converting consultancy sites

Section 06
Lead-Gen Features to Build
Features that convert website visitors into qualified leads and paying clients. Red dot = build first. Amber = build next. Green = nice-to-have once the basics are in place.
Direct Calendar Booking (Calendly / Cal.com)
Embed a "Book Free 30-min AI Audit" calendar directly in the homepage hero and contact page. Removes all friction from the #1 conversion action. Eliminates email back-and-forth. Kalvium Labs and SynkrAI both treat this as their primary lead capture mechanism — it converts 3–5x better than a contact form alone. Cal.com is free to start.
Case Study Pages with Hard Metrics
Even 2 anonymised case studies ("E-commerce client in Mumbai: 62% reduction in order processing time, 3-week delivery") will significantly reduce bounce rate on service pages. Standard format: client industry → problem → solution → timeline → measurable outcome. Use a consistent visual card template. This is the content investment with the highest conversion ROI for any consultancy.
Free Lead Magnet (Gated PDF Download)
Offer a downloadable resource: "AI Readiness Scorecard for Indian SMBs" or "Top 10 Automation Wins Under 30 Days" in exchange for name + email. Even 200 email subscribers gives you: retargeting audience, nurture email sequence, and direct sales outreach list. This is your long-term most valuable asset — email leads who weren't ready to buy today will become clients in 3–6 months.
ROI / Savings Calculator Widget
Interactive tool: "How many hours does your team spend on [task] per week?" × team size × average salary = estimated annual cost → "We can cut this by 40% → ₹X saved per year." End with: "Book a call to verify this estimate." Creates personalised urgency, is highly shareable on LinkedIn, and makes the value of your services concrete rather than abstract. Can be built with basic JavaScript.
Live Chat / AI Chat Agent on the Website
Add a website chat widget (Crisp free tier, Intercom Starter, or a custom Claude-powered chatbot). Pre-answer FAQs: "What's your pricing?" "Which industries do you serve?" "How long does a project take?" Capture name + email before handing off. If you're building AI solutions for clients, having one on your own site is also powerful social proof — you're eating your own cooking.
Blog with SEO-Targeted Articles
Publish 2 articles/month targeting long-tail, high-intent keywords: "AI chatbot for Indian legal firms," "marketing automation for SME India," "how to integrate CRM with ERP using AI," "generative AI for manufacturing quality assurance." Organic search is the most cost-effective lead channel at startup stage — and these keywords have low competition. Results visible in 3–6 months.
Real Client Logo Strip
Replace the "Trusted by innovative startups" text claim with 4–6 real client logos (even SMB clients). Logos work as social proof at zero reading effort — the brain registers authority immediately. If NDA constraints apply, use "Client in Legal Services" placeholder cards with industry icons and a quote. Any visual proof beats a text claim.
Exit-Intent Popup with Lead Capture
When a first-time visitor moves to close the tab, trigger a modal: "Before you go — get your free AI Readiness Report." Offer the gated PDF. Target only first-time visitors to avoid annoying returning visitors. Converts 3–8% of would-be exits into email captures — high ROI for essentially zero cost. Tools: Poptin free tier or a custom JS mouseleave event listener.
LinkedIn Social Proof Screenshots
Embed 2–3 cropped LinkedIn recommendation screenshots as image testimonials on the homepage and service pages. Link to the recommender's LinkedIn profile for verifiability. LinkedIn testimonials carry significantly higher trust than anonymous web reviews in B2B settings because they're publicly verifiable — the buyer can click through and see the recommender is a real person at a real company.
